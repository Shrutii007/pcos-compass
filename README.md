# 🌸 PCOS Compass

> A free, private, and compassionate guide for women navigating PCOS — no login, no API key, no cost.

---

## 💜 Why I Built This — A Personal Story

I have PCOS. And for a long time, I thought I was broken.

At my worst, I would go 2 to 3 months without a period. That's one of the most severe presentations of PCOS — a sign that my body's hormonal system was significantly disrupted. I felt exhausted, defeated, and completely disconnected from my own body. The brain fog, the weight gain, the anxiety — it wasn't just physical. It was isolating. I genuinely believed I would never get better.

Then in April 2024, something shifted. I decided — no matter what — I was going to fight for my health.

I started going to the gym every single day, even if it was just for 30 minutes. I cut out junk food and sugar. I stopped waiting for a miracle and started showing up for myself, consistently, even on the days I didn't want to.

One year later, I had lost 16 kg. I went from 70 kg down to 54 kg. My periods, which used to disappear for months at a time, now come every 45 days. That's not a perfect cycle — but it's a transformation. My mental health improved dramatically too. The anxiety that used to cloud every single day started to lift. I felt like myself again — energetic, clear-headed, and genuinely hopeful for the first time in years.

I'm not fully cured. PCOS doesn't work like that. But I am proof that consistency, even imperfect consistency, changes everything.

I built PCOS Compass because I wish something like this had existed when I was at my lowest. I didn't need a doctor's appointment I couldn't afford or a medical journal I couldn't understand. I needed someone — or something — to explain what was happening in my body, tell me I wasn't alone, and show me what I could actually do about it.

That's what this app is.

## 🎯 Who This Is For

PCOS Compass was built for every woman who has ever felt dismissed, confused, or alone in her PCOS journey. Specifically:

**Women who are undiagnosed or recently diagnosed**
They're experiencing symptoms — irregular periods, acne, hair changes, weight gain — but don't know what's happening. They turn to Google and get overwhelmed or scared. PCOS Compass gives them a clear, calm starting point.

**Women in underserved or low-income communities**
Specialist appointments are expensive and often have months-long waitlists. Many women can't afford to see an endocrinologist or gynecologist every time they have a question. PCOS Compass is completely free, available 24/7, and requires nothing but a browser.

**College students and young women**
Many women develop PCOS symptoms in their teens or early twenties and have no idea what they're experiencing. On a college campus, access to quality reproductive health information is often limited or buried. This tool meets them where they are.

**Women who have been dismissed by doctors**
PCOS takes an average of 2+ years to diagnose. Many women are told their symptoms are normal, that they just need to "lose weight," or that their concerns aren't serious. PCOS Compass takes every symptom seriously and empowers women to advocate for themselves in medical settings.

**Women supporting someone with PCOS**
Partners, parents, and friends who want to understand what their loved one is going through can use PCOS Compass to learn in a compassionate, jargon-free environment.

## 🌸 How It Helps

### 🔍 Symptom Checker Quiz
An 8-question interactive quiz that walks women through the most common PCOS symptoms — menstrual irregularity, acne, hirsutism (facial/body hair), scalp hair loss, weight, mood, fatigue, and insulin resistance. At the end, it gives a personalized Low / Moderate / High likelihood result with:
- A clear explanation of what the result means
- A summary of the user's reported symptoms
- Specific, actionable next steps tailored to their result

This is not a diagnostic tool — but it is a starting point that helps women walk into a doctor's appointment informed and prepared, rather than scared and uncertain.

### 📚 Info Hub
Six evidence-based information cards that break down the most important PCOS topics in plain, warm language:

- **Diet & Nutrition** — what to eat, what to avoid, and why the low-glycemic approach works
- **Treatment Options** — lifestyle changes, common medications, and what to discuss with a doctor
- **Mental Health** — why PCOS causes anxiety and depression, and what actually helps
- **Fertility & PCOS** — the truth about getting pregnant with PCOS, and the full range of options
- **Understanding Lab Results** — what LH, FSH, testosterone, AMH, and insulin tests actually mean
- **Exercise Guide** — the best types of movement for PCOS and why over-exercising can make things worse

### 💥 Myth Buster
Six of the most harmful PCOS myths — debunked with facts. Because misinformation causes real harm. Women deserve the truth.

## 🧠 The Mental Health Dimension

PCOS is not just a reproductive condition. It is a full-body hormonal and metabolic condition that profoundly affects mental health — and this is one of the most under-discussed aspects of the disease.

Women with PCOS are 3x more likely to experience anxiety and depression than women without it. The hormonal imbalances — particularly elevated androgens and insulin resistance — directly affect serotonin and dopamine regulation. Add to that the emotional weight of visible symptoms like acne, hair loss, and weight changes, and the grief of fertility uncertainty, and it becomes clear why so many women with PCOS also struggle mentally.

PCOS Compass acknowledges this throughout. Every piece of content is written with emotional awareness — not just clinical facts. Because healing isn't only physical. It's feeling understood, feeling less alone, and believing that things can get better.

They can. I am living proof.
